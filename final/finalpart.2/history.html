The Founding of Yellowstone

Yellowstone became the world's first national park in 1872 to preserve its unique geothermal features and wildlife. This pioneering step laid the groundwork for conservation efforts around the world. Source

The Hayden Expedition

The 1871 Hayden Geological Survey brought national attention to Yellowstone. Their reports and photographs influenced Congress to protect this remarkable landscape. Source

Legacy and Impact

Yellowstone set the stage for a global conservation movement. Today, it remains a symbol of nature's beauty and the importance of environmental preservation. Source
