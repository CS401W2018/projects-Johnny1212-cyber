Quick Facts About Yellowstone

  • First national park in the world (established 1872) National Park Service
  • Home to over 10,000 geothermal features National Park Service - Geothermal
  • Located in Wyoming, Montana, and Idaho Yellowstone National Park
  • More than 4 million visitors per year Visitor Statistics - National Park Service
  • Rich in wildlife: bison, bears, wolves, elk, and more Wildlife - National Park Service

Geysers and Hot Springs

Yellowstone is famous for its geothermal wonders, including the iconic Old Faithful geyser and the Grand Prismatic Spring. These natural features result from the park sitting atop a volcanic hot spot, making it one of the most geologically active areas on Earth. Source

Wildlife Haven

From grizzly bears to bison herds, Yellowstone is a sanctuary for diverse animal species. It provides critical habitat for many North American wildlife and plays a key role in conservation efforts. Source

Adventure Awaits

Whether you love hiking, camping, photography, or wildlife watching, Yellowstone offers unforgettable experiences. With miles of trails and stunning views, it's a paradise for outdoor enthusiasts of all ages. Source
